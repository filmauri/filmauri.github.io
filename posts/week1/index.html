First things change

Posted on Feb 20, 2022

Changes

A lot has been changing in the last while about how I use social. They’ve always been a way to take my mind off things and get into a parallel world of ad, gifted and shit. I was starting to feel like it was time wasted so I started decluttering through my instagram followings, the only social I had. I wanted to make sure I was following my friends and profiles that could add value to the time I was spending on social media and not take away from it. I tried but the result was not the best, the “Explore” section is just a black hole that draws you in and out after half an hour (when it’s good) not even knowing your name. So I decided to uninstall Instagram. Now I only use it for filippo.im. Now the social I use the most is twitter. Its “Explore” section is also done well. But for real. It shows you what people are talking about around the world and what the “hot topics” of the moment are. Also the type of posts and people using twitter helps you use social as a means of information and building a personal opinion about the world. I then discovered medium, a social where the posts are articles about whatever. Truly a great idea that of medium. If I had known about it before, maybe instead of opening a blog and spending a thousand hours tweaking it to get it to go the way I wanted, I would have just made an account on medium and I would have been on my way. There is also a comments section on the articles where you can discuss. Never have I found comments to be much more useful than the article itself. I read an article about how some guy, Joash Xu, obviously unknown, was taking notes. It seemed like a very organized thing and I was already downloading all the apps he used to be able to match such perfection and organization. Reading the comments I see that one word is often repeated. Obisidian, Obsidian, Obsidian. And I say, beautiful there, what is it? A world opened up to me. How the heck had I never heard that before? I had no idea and yet the world around it is gigantic. Just think, I’m writing this article right now in Obisidian. I suggest you to have a look at it because it has turned my note-taking around (LaTex will never leave you, though…). It has a lot of crazy features that you can discover in this video. I discovered the Zettelkasten, a way of taking notes to remember things better. In short, a great app that I’ve already had a chance to recommend to my friends.

I’ve also had a lot of other falls in love like blockchain and world NFT, app development, ML (which I’m doing in school), university. There will be time to talk about everything. Now it’s off to bed.

Well he’s a good essayist and in this book he talks about “How we stopped experiencing the real”.

Have a great week!!!
