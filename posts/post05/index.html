News from Space

Posted on Apr 23, 2021

You’ve probably heard of Perseverance, the NASA rover that landed on Mars on February 18. Along with it came a small drone called Ingenuity that made history on April 19. In fact, never before that day had a craft managed to hover over another planet. Due to the distance from Mars, scientists monitoring the drone had to wait more than 3 hours to get the data from the experiment. Such a great distance in fact does not allow the flying of the drone, even the transmitted light reflected from Mars takes tens of minutes to reach us. What flew the drone was an artificial intelligence installed inside it. Ingenuity’s function is to prove that it is possible to fly on Mars. If it can make 5 successful flights, the small Martian drone will start to push further and further away from the crater from which it started. With this flight the exploration of Mars begins.

On Mars there are many caves that could be explored only by a drone. In this way through radar and spectrometers could analyze the environment and the rocks present. These tunnels, which protect from radiation are the most likely places for the discovery of alien life! Before we get to that, of course we have to prove that we can fly. The telemetry revealed that all the passages happened correctly and even photos taken by Perseverance arrived on Earth!

It may seem like a normal drone flight as many times happens on earth but it is not so! The difficulty of this flight is very high. The atmosphere of Mars is one hundred times less dense than that of Earth and the temperatures are prohibitive. Mars has an average temperature of about -63 ° C, think of your iPhone when the temperature is close to zero it freezes … Just think, when NASA was planning this mission they said they wouldn’t manage to fly Ingenuity on Mars. In fact, the project was taken over by JPL as a demonstration of their engineering capabilities.

This short flight may be one of the small steps that will lead our civilization to be multiplanetary as Elon Musk has been dreaming of for many years now. On Friday morning Ingenuity made its second flight and managed to reach greater heights and perform maneuvers never done before.

But that’s not all that has happened on the distant red planet. In fact, MIT and its engineers have managed to synthesize breathable oxygen from CO2 in Mars' atmosphere. After solving the problem of oxygen, there is little left to guarantee life on Mars. Now we only need some clothes to keep us warm during the freezing winters on Mars that reach -133°C and some food.
