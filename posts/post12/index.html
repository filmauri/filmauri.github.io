Time to become better

Posted on Aug 19, 2021

​

Summer finally arrives and it’s already over. Well come on, there’s still quite a while to go before we have to return to the daily grind. We still have a lot of free time to make use of. It’s in free time that one really understands what one wants. Vacation is the noblest time of the year, because it’s the time when one either commits to what he wants with the value he recognizes as prevalent in his life or he doesn’t commit to anything at all and then it’s a fool’s errand.

For sure, everyone wants to improve themselves every year, it’s undeniable. Summer is the privileged time to do so. A fundamental habit is reading. For over a year now, I have incorporated reading into the routine of my days. With so much time on my hands, summer is the best way to start doing it. You’ll instantly see the benefits!

Thinking about students like me, I have found that the more I read, the faster I understand. This has helped in the past and will certainly remain an important skill in the future. In fact, it’s easier to take in information and act on it.

But it’s not just something functional. First and foremost, it’s something enjoyable

Enjoying a compelling novel while sitting on your balcony before going to sleep is one of the best things in the world. It’s an activity that can be very relaxing, depending of course on the books you choose to read. In fact, a recent study found that just six minutes of reading has been shown to reduce muscle tension and even slow down the heart rate of readers. Overall, it reduced stress levels by an impressive 68%. If that’s not enough to convince you of the importance of reading, then we don’t know what will!

Many men and women were tested for memory and thinking every year in their later years to keep track of their progress. After their deaths, autopsies showed that those who had engaged in activities such as reading and writing had a slower rate of memory decline than those who had not read.

Another reason reading is important is that it allows for a broader perspective on the world and others. Reading can take us out of our closed bubble, allowing us to see a different view of the world, one outside of our own. In this way we will be prepared to have our say on a great many topics. In fact, reading allows you to form your own opinion about the things being told and what is happening in the world now.
